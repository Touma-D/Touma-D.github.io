Research Projects

Our group conducts cutting-edge research in building energy systems, HVAC optimization, and intelligent building technologies. Below is a selection of major ongoing and completed projects.

ARPA‑E SENSOR Project

Development of advanced sensing and control technologies for next‑generation HVAC systems.

NSF IN2WIBE Project

Investigation of intelligent and interactive building environments with human‑centric design.

DOE Building America Project

Improving residential building energy efficiency through innovative HVAC and envelope solutions.

Water Source Heat Pump (WSHP) Testbed

Experimental platform for evaluating advanced WSHP performance and control strategies.

DOE CYDRES Project

Research on cyber‑resilient HVAC systems and secure building control architectures.

DOE IEDO Project

Energy optimization and decarbonization strategies for commercial building HVAC systems.

NSF PIRE Project

International collaboration on building energy flexibility and climate‑adaptive design.

DOE DWHP Project

Development of dual‑source heat pump technologies for high‑efficiency building applications.
